Empower Your Shower

The Solution

It doesn't take any brain power to save water, just a little creative thinking!

To Save Water at Home:

1. Take shorter showers.

2. It takes about 70 gallons of water to fill a bathtub, so showers are the more water-efficient way to bathe.

3. Use energy efficient appliances.

4. A cup of coffee takes 55 gallons of water to make, with most of that H2O used to grow the coffee beans. So cut back on it (you really don't need the caffeine).

5. The average pool takes 22,000 gallons of water to fill, and if you don't cover it, hundreds of gallons of water per month can be lost due to evaporation.

6. Use a broom, not a hose, to clean driveways and sidewalks.

7. Exchange lawn with gravel or culitvate plant species that are native to your region.

8. Eat less meat! Meat is very expensive in terms or virtual water.

9. Take care of that leaky faucet! Leaks can result in 4-6 gallons wasted per day.

10. Finally, never leave the faucet running.
